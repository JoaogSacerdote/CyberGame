Abstract. This paper presents the ongoing development of CyberSec: Network
Defender, an interactive embedded gaming console designed to teach cyberse-
curity fundamentals through gamification. The escalating volume of cyber thre-
ats and the increasing digital dependence of society make information-security
literacy a critical competency for citizens of all ages; nevertheless, educational
contexts frequently address these topics through abstract theoretical methods
that hinder comprehension and engagement. Grounded in game-based lear-
ning, experiential learning theory, and constructivism, the project employs the
ESP32-S3 dual-core microcontroller, the FreeRTOS real-time operating system,
and the LVGL embedded graphics library to create a top-down game scenario
that simulates the defence of a corporate IT infrastructure under cyberattack.
A central mechanic involves three physical NFC defence cards, each encoding
a specific cybersecurity countermeasure: network isolation, emergency backup
restoration, and load balancing against denial-of-service attacks. These phy-
sical interactions materialise abstract security concepts such as authentication
and disaster recovery. The current development stage encompasses the finalisa-
tion of the graphical interface in a local LVGL/SDL2 simulator environment, the
production of pixel-art game sprites, and the assembly of electronic hardware
components. The approach demonstrates the potential of low-cost embedded
hardware to democratise access to interactive pedagogical tools across diverse
educational settings.
